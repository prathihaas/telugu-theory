Our story

One grandmother. Two stoves. Thirteen spices.

Telugu Theory began where most good restaurants do — in a home, with an argument about salt. Our founder Keerthi took her grandmother's recipe book, kept the recipes, and threw out the shortcuts. What's left is Andhra and Telangana food as it was meant to be: slow, loud, and served without apology.

We mill our own rice flour, press our own gingelly oil, and cure our avakaya pickle every March in clay jars. The chilli is from Guntur. The tamarind is from Anantapur. The goat, when it is goat, is from a farmer two hours south.
