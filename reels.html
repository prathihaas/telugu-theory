Chapter 07 · Reels Playbook

Natural reels.
Shot like us.

A shot-by-shot system for filming Telugu Theory reels — real food, real hands, real light. Branding shows up in the world we shoot, not as overlays. Built to be unmistakable in a feed and never trip Meta's reach signals.

Five kinds of reel.

Every shoot fits one of these. Pick the pillar before you pick up the phone — it tells you the lens, the light, and how long to roll.

PILLAR 01

The dish.

One plate, one slow camera move. The hero is the food, in its own light.

PILLAR 02

The making.

Hands, fire, pestle, dough. Texture and rhythm. Sound is the action itself.

PILLAR 03

The arrival.

Plate hits table, lid lifts, steam rises. Six seconds, no words.

PILLAR 04

The recipe.

Three steps, three shots. Ends on the dish, not on a graphic.

PILLAR 05

The voice.

Chef or regular speaks one line. Captioned, no music under speech.

Three reels, frame by frame.

Each storyboard is shoot-ready: timecodes, shot type, what's in frame, what to listen for.

House rules for shooting.

Same phone, same lens, same light direction every time. Consistency lives in the craft, not in graphics.

Camera

Phone, but disciplined.

  • iPhone 15 Pro · 1× lens for everything except overheads (0.5×).
  • Vertical 1080×1920, 30fps. Never landscape, never 24fps cinematic.
  • Tripod or gimbal, always. No handheld unless the sway is part of the shot.
  • Locked exposure + focus by tap-and-hold. Auto-exposure ruins the dum reveal.
  • One move per shot — push in, tilt down, or hold. Never two.
Light

One source, warm.

  • Pass lamp (warm tungsten) is the house key light. Never overhead fluorescents.
  • North-facing window for daytime prep shoots. Soft, neutral.
  • Dark surround. Black tablecloth or wood under hero shots.
  • Underexpose by ⅓ stop — keeps reds rich, gold spice deep.
  • No ring light. Ever. Reads "TikTok," not "restaurant."
Sound

Diegetic first.

  • Record the action. Sizzle, thump, snap, pour. That's the track.
  • Voice = phone close. 6 inches from mouth, on a separate device.
  • Music sparingly. One tabla loop from Instagram's audio library if needed — never trending pop.
  • No copyright music. Mutes the reel for half the planet.
  • Burn captions in for any spoken line. Half of viewers watch on mute.
